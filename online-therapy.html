Is Online Therapy Right for You?

As the mental health community grows so has its ability to reach those that struggled to meet with therapists in their area. People that once struggled to book an appointment or had not considered in-person therapy due to social anxiety can now stay at home in bed and meet with a therapist. This greatly expands patients options to get treatment on mobile devices through apps such as BetterHelp, 7Cups, Lyra, etc. However as online therapy grows, incidents have caused people to question the effectiveness of online therapy such as the situation with BetterHelp in 2018 when its advertisers profited from those with mental illnesses (Matas, 2018). As online therapy grows and becomes a more popular option for those seeking treatment is it for everyone?

Studies

Multiple studies have shown no significant differences between online therapy versus face-to-face therapy (Wagner (2014), Andrews (2018), and Nordgren (2014)). There have even been studies conducted that provide significant evidence for online therapy being slightly more effective than face-to-face therapy for those who are specifically seeking Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (Sarkis (2020), Luo (2020)).

PROS

  • No commute
  • Ability to more freely communicate
  • More variety of therapist
  • No waiting rooms for those with social anxiety
  • No formal wear

CONS

  • Unable to be effective for certain conditions
  • Technological issues
  • Therapists will have a more difficult time creating a therapeutic alliance
  • Some advertised people may not be licensed
  • Difficulties intervening on a crisis

Personal Experience

Throughout my mental health journey I have been to both online and face-to-face therapy and prefer online therapy to face-to-face. I am not restricted to meeting with my therapist at a certain location and am able to schedule a call with whatever works with their availability. It has also allowed me to stay with the same therapist no matter where I go; for example, if I go on a vacation (like I am doing currently writing this) I will not have to postpone my therapy sessions. This has allowed me the freedom and helped my mental health journey as I used to move quite frequently and did not like to open up so frequently to new therapists. My therapist is super nice and though there are technical errors or sometimes static, I am still able to get the same quality and even better care by doing online therapy versus my experience doing face-to-face therapy.
